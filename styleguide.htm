General Rules

Titles

The first and last words of the title should always be capitalized. All main words (nouns, verbs, pronouns, adjectives, and adverbs) will also be capitalized. The following rules apply to when we do not capitalize a word.

  1. Prepositions, determiners, interjections, and conjunctions (coordinating/subordinating)
  2. Auxilliary Verbs (regular use of 'to be', do, have, can, will, etc.) and their contractions
  3. The preceding categories can be capitalized when they start a new phrase with equal weight within a title (i.e. "To My Mother, To My Father")
