4-Way Subway Station

Subway Station Schematic
(Lower Level, y = 55)
Subway Station Schematic
(Upper Level, y = 58)

The subway system in Skunkland is at level 55 so the lower level schematic is at elevation 55. The upper level schematic is at level 58. Rails with a green background are one level up (so level 56 in the lower schematic). Rails with a red background are one level down (so level 57 in the upper schematic). The wooden planks that make up the boarding area in the upper schematic are at level 59 (and the rails over them). Don't forget the redstone dust and activator buttons above the boarding area. Each station's boarding area also comes with a chest containing at least 2 minecarts. Make sure that the powered rail in the boarding area is not powered (so that it brakes the arriving minecart). The two schematics show standard placement for torches and the upper schematic shows placement for the three switches.

It is critical that the station is built in the correct orientation (these schematics are oriented to the north) because of the SE Rule in Minecraft. Essentially the rule means that when a minecart rolls onto a section of track perpendicular to it (i.e. hits a 'T') it will automatically go South (if it's a North-South track) or East (if it's a West-East track).


New Compact 4-Way Subway Station

Compact Subway Station Schematic
(Lower Level, y = 55)
Compact Subway Station Schematic
(Upper Level, y = 58)

This schematic changes the old subway slightly by moving the North access line 3m West (to line it up with the South access line) and the East access line 2m South (to line it up with the West access line) and the redirect loop only travels 1m (instead of 2m) West now. The West and South access lines remain unchanged. Note the N-S alignment of the rail that the West and East accesses roll into; if it is allowed to curve one of the access routes will not work.

The compact upper section also shows a simplified switching system if you intend to only use two switches (true binary) instead of the standard 3. This method requires some redstone dust to allow the right switch to control both track intersections.
