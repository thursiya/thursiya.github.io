January/March 2014

*** The New Kingsburg Monitor ***

Announcement: Historians, biographers, and chroniclers of Skunkland, the New Kingsburg Monitor is looking for all parties with an interest in the names and places of Skunkland to contribute the lore of their land. We hope to compile it and share it far and wide, in the spirit of our journalistic tradition. We are particularly looking for place names, flags and other national/sub-national emblems, general demographics, and cultural information.

Our server admin finally had a day off so we get that long-awaited newsletter. We are currently in version 1.7.8, presumably the final update before the big shift into 1.8 (which should finally allow players to change their names). New builders have been carving out mountains and caves while a few old builders have returned and life goes on in our third year in Skunkland.

Our most active new builder is jaanke, who has claimed the lofty peaks southeast of the Koku Kingdom. Jaanke and nebby28 have constructed some fast mob-grinders for players who want to level up quickly and hopefully will share with us their construction secrets. The two biggest builds in the previous months have been nebby's fortress and (unnamed) city, and spaceskunks' secret tower. Spaceskunks has also expanded the borders of Delve south and finished Castle Gorllewin (with its impressive, functioning portcullis). Thursiya completed the construction of the Doggy Magical headquarters in Mellon Bay and has begun work on the Ottokar Fortress. Msteveharrison has moved into the eastern regions of South Shore to construct the western-themed Cutter's Junction. The town of Mysteria has been renamed Braille. To help facilitate jaanke's new build the subway has expanded south from the moon base to a new station in southeast Koku and east from there to jaanke's base. It has also expanded north from Stellarton to Noricum.

There are lots of things to see in this build roundup. Featured Build: Porcinia

Porcinia, the second commune of Skunkland, was founded in 2013 by aninabanina. As a classic example of a new crafter's first build the town has gone through a number of changes over its short life. The original cobblestone mining shed has been replaced by a full-fledged farm and homestead, the first tower of Porcinia stands as an historical ruin already, original hardscrabble farms have been replaced by a fancy auto-farm, and the town has been repopulated numerous times as its hardy pioneers face the grim onslaught of the terrors of the night. The town of Porcinia is notable for a very fast initial build and for housing the first exotic flowers in Skunkland. Make sure to check out Ida's Flower Shop, Porcinia Station, the new seaside homes, and venture across the giant wooden bridge in the northeast to explore an old abandoned build. Porcinia can be reached by road or subway east of South Shore.
