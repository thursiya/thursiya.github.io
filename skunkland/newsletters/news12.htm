July/August 2014

*** The New Kingsburg Monitor ***

Announcement: Yard Sale in Ghibli Port. Sailors from Ghibli Port will be selling excess oak doors and oak fences to help finance an expedition to explore ocean monuments in the north Levantian Ocean. Donations welcome. Expedition plundering not welcome.

Time for the great door burning! 1.8 - "The Bountiful Update" is out and it's bringing quite a few changes. This was the longest wait for an update yet (nearly a year). We are currently in version 1.8, so expect a few hiccups server-side as the world adapts to the new code. Important changes in 1.8:
- New Doors! (all 6 wood types), iron trapdoor, plus all wood types now available for fences and gates
- Beautiful, Customizeable Banners! - Ocean Monuments (also spawned in old terrain) filled with dangerous Guardians and fancy prismarine, sponges, and sea lanterns
- Red Sandstone, Diorite, Andesite, and Granite
- Slime Blocks (mainly for extending the possibilities of redstone circuits)
- Armor Stands
- New food (Rabbit Stew, Roast Mutton and Roast Rabbit) and food changes (Carrots and Baked Potatoes now less effective)
- Mossy, Cracked, and Chiseled Stone Bricks (and Moss Stone) can now be crafted
- All Mob Heads are now available (from mobs killed by creeper blasts)
- Furnace smelting now "dies out" when fuel runs out
- Items can now be rotated in 8 directions in Item Frames
- Buttons can now be placed on the ground or ceiling
- Daylight Sensors can now be inverted (on at night) by right-clicking them
- Signs and Written Books now have new character limits (based on size)
- A number of changes to Enchanting (including needing lapis)
- Villagers have updated trades and now harvest their own food

It's been a while since we've had a regular newsletter update. Most of the work this summer can be attributed to Shylilie and Spaceskunks. Shylilie has move ahead full steam building her future capital city of Auverland and it already includes many stately buildings and Shylilie is open to other players' requests for building sites. After completing a small residential expansion on the western outskirts of Delve, Spaceskunks has headed into the Swamp of Joy to build the world's first witch spawner, and then a little further south to start construction of the nordic settlement Ulve. On another break from constructing the Ottokar Fortress, thursiya has completed the Century Tower condominiums in Southside New Kingsburg to celebrate the year 200. New player Quackey958 has been busy expanding the Badrum Imperium, including the large, walled capital city and the village of Alf-Wy. Quackey958 has also cut a subway route from the Badrum capital all the way to Braille (over 1km) and there are new connections west from Cadwgan to Jungle Tours and east from Century Build to Southside New Kingsburg.

Have you missed build updates? We've got a few choice sites to show you. Clockwise from top left: Winona Mim's Hut (Ennist province, Delve); Century Tower (New Kingsburg); Marshway Drive residential expansion (Delve City); Horse Track (north Faclair); Ulve downtown (Ennist province, Delve); Marchelund Centre and Auverland Public Library (west Faclair); Alf-Wy (east Badrum).


New Server and World Maintenance

On August 15th our server went down due to an error in the roll-out of MCMA's latest update (MCMA is the server control software we use that our server host leases). We eventually had to solve the issue by starting a new server but fortunately all of our world files were intact and no work was lost. If you haven't done so already, please update your server info to the new address: sc26.servercraft.co:21746

With the release of 1.8 there are new blocks spawned during world generation so users have discussed the possibility of doing another world trim to have closer access to the new blocks. We are looking for any input regarding this proposal (agree, disagree, possible trimlines). Barring user input admin will go ahead with trimming a small portion of the world map. Another issue to discuss is the possibility of nerfing mob terrain damage - meaning that endermen and creepers would no longer alter the environment.


Century Build Competition

Thanks to all of the participants who came out during the year 200 to build a statue for the first Century Build Competition. Shylilie started things off by constructing a large, lime green creeper and then encouraged players to remove blocks from the creeper until it revealed her final statue, "The Faraway Tree", underneath. Quackey958 created an unnamed statue representing the elements with a distinct flavour of the Badrum Imperium. Spaceskunks and thursiya both preconstructed their designs offline so Spaceskunks' "Existence" and thursiya's "Ad Astra" (out of competition due to thursiya representing the host, Operis Corporation) went up very quickly. The winner of the competition was a very tough decision, considering the impressive variety of entrants, and was declared msteveharrison & Aninabanina's "Porksicle" which did an exceptional job of capturing their whimsical build vision. Participants were awarded prize packages including making their marks on a forthcoming Operis Corporation construction project in New Kingsburg.


Featured Build: Moon Base

Back in November of 2013 players msteveharrison and Aninabanina were starting what would become the first of many cooperatively constructed projects. They swiftly completed the Moon Base that December. The Moon Base was one of the first structures on the server to be made with the newly available stained glass and remains one of the largest structures built to date, clearly showing up on all subsequent maps. It is a good representation of an active building process, incorporating various building necessities in one location (food production, mines, etc.). When it was decided that subway access was preferable, new connections were quickly built and the Moon Base station helped spur the southern expansion of the subway network. Besides constructing the base they also had to level and re-cover a large chunk of land with sand to give the Moon Base an appropriately lunar setting. Make sure to check out the fountain rest area, sliding "space" doors, and hidden away rooms. The Moon Base is only reachable by subway (there is no exterior access) east from Lunar Hill or south from South Shore.
