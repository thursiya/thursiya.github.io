Crosslinguistic Analysis of Possessive Particles

Chinese - 的

  1. Possessive (NP.Possessor 的 NP.Possessed) (This is NP 的)
  2. Link a noun/adjective/phrase to a noun to describe it (AP.2+Syllables 的 NP) (NP.Descriptor 的 NP) (VP 的 NP - Go Beijing 的 Train -> THe train that goes to Beijing)
  3. Nominalizer (A 的 -> NP) (Number CLS 的 -> NP - I want 2 CLS 3 CLS.Mao 的 -> I want 2 of the 3.10cent/30cent ones)
  4. Stress a sentence element (V 的) (V 的 NP.Object)
  5. Sentence final particle indicating emphasis
  6. "Of that kind" (NP 的) (Sewing.Notions 的 - things like sewing notions)
  7. Informal: Multiplication/Addition

Japanese - の

  1. Genetive Case Marker (NP.Possessor の NP.Possessed)
  2. Identity/Apposition (N.Title の NP) (NP の NP.Attributive)
  3. A Noun Modifier (Field の Mathematics -> The field of mathematics)
  4. Sentence final particle indicating emphasis/question, depending on intonation
  5. Nominalizes an adjective, verb, or phrase (To.Eat の I like -> I like eating)

French - 'de'

  1. Possessive (Belonging) (NP.Possessed de NP.Possessor) (Paris est la capitale de la France)
  2. Possessive (Property or Association) (la femme de mon ami)
  3. Origin (Elle vient de France) (Le train va de Paris à Bordeaux)
  4. of (indicates an amount) (5 kilos de pommes)
  5. Attributive (jus de pomme) (voiture de sport)
  6. Origin.Time (je travaille de 8:00 à midi)
  7. Used after certain verbs before an infinitive (j'ai arrêté de fumer)
  8. by (boire 3 tasses par jour réduirait de 20% les risques)
  9. Article: Some; any (in questions or negatives) (je voudrais de la viande)
  10. Article: (negative) a, an, any (elle n'a pas de mère)
