Modality & Truth Values
Particle Validity Meaning
-not False Negative form, negates the word
-? Unsure Interrogative form, questioning the word
-! Definite Emphatic form, emphasizing the word

The modality particles can be added to any word. Specifically when modifying the sense of an entire sentence the particle is added to the head verb:

You Go -? School
you are going to question a school
"Are you going to school?"

You Go -not School
you are going to negation a school
"You are not going to school."

Combining an interrogative clause with a resultative clause creates the 'if-then' scenario. The conjunction 'Then' introduces a following resultative clause that evaluates to true if the preceding clause evaluates to true. An 'else' form can follow the negated conjunction 'Then' (or be created by negating a pro-form of the interrogative clause and following with the 'Then' conjunction, effectively reversing the sentence's polarity).

You Go -not -? School Then Ø Go -! Work
you are going to negation question a school if-then you are going to emphasis a workplace
"If you are not going to school then you must go to work."

That Then -not Ø Forget -not Books REL You
pro-sentence if-then negation you forget negation books relation you(r)
"(Maybe you are not going to school) otherwise don't forget your books."
